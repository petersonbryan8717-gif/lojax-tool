Operating Strategy

A structured approach to building a profitable, scalable Touch Coffee operation across Westchester and Putnam County.

Placement Architecture

Anchors vs. Fillers

  • Target 2–3 high-volume anchors (DMV, hospital, sports complex) that cover all fixed costs
  • Add filler locations for pure margin — they require minimal effort once established
  • Never rely on a single anchor for more than 40% of total revenue
  • Prioritize locations where you can place a 2nd machine as volume grows
Pricing Strategy

Tier Your Prices

  • Premium ($5.50–6.50): Greenwich Equinox, Sportime Armonk, airport, luxury dealers
  • Standard ($4.50–5.50): Hospitals, corporate, DMV, pickleball
  • Value ($3.50–4.50): Planet Fitness, senior center, school events
  • One price for all locations leaves significant revenue on the table
Operations

Backup & Downtime Plan

  • Establish a same-day response commitment to venue managers — it protects the relationship
  • Identify a local technician contact before you need one, not after
  • Consider a 3rd machine as a "hot spare" once you have 5+ active placements
  • Track machine uptime per location — downtime kills trust faster than anything
Agreements

Protect Your Placements

  • Get written agreements from day one — even a simple 1-page letter of understanding
  • Include a 90-day notice clause for termination — protects both sides fairly
  • Where possible, have a secondary contact at each venue beyond the decision-maker
  • Renewal clauses with 90-day auto-renewal are your friend
Revenue Growth

Ad Screen Revenue

  • The Touch machine's 21.5" screen is a second revenue stream — don't ignore it
  • Pitch local businesses near each location (gyms → protein bars, hospitals → pharmacies)
  • $100–300/mo per screen in local ad revenue is realistic within 6 months
  • Your Chamber VP friend is the perfect intro to local advertisers
Scaling

Machine 3 & 4 Triggers

  • Order machine 3 when machines 1 & 2 together net $2,000+/mo profit for 60 days
  • Order machine 4 when machine 3 breaks even within 90 days of placement
  • Target a new cluster with each new machine — don't stack machines in the same cluster
  • 10 machines is a full-time operation — plan your time threshold in advance
